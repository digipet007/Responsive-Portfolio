About Me

I am a programmer in the Seattle area. After completing the University of Washington's Full Stack Flex program, I hope to find a challenging and impactful role that will allow me to make a positive contribution to the world while continuing to develop my expertise. For the past six years, I have worked as an educator in the public school setting in the Chicago area, teaching a variety of subjects, ranging from fifth grade sheltered instruction to ESL. My background in education has provided me with an applied skillset in project management and collaborating with a diverse team of professionals. After of years of service within the community, I have developed an interest in creating accessible technology for education. I have a Master's in Education from Dominican University with a focus in educational technology and an endorsement as a Technology Specialist, which has given me a firm foundation in the application of technology to diversify instruction and provide access to the curriculum. I have a Bachelor's in Language, Literacy, and Cultural Studies, with a minor in Teaching English as a Second Language from Western Washington University. When I am not programming or teaching, I can be found hiking, painting, reading, and playing Dungeons and Dragons with my fiance. Check out our collaborative Dungeons and Dragons story on DmsGuild!
